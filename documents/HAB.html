Habitability in Thousand Parsec Proposal

Abstract

This document talks about how the habitability of a planet is determined. It talks about the major and minor factors and related topics such as terra-forming. This document aims to give races a wide range of flexibility in selecting their preferred planetary environment without being impossibly complex.


Introduction


There are 2 important factors about a planet that concern the player. The first is how fast your population grows on a planet, the second is how many people you can fit on it. Both these factors are important in determining how good a planet is to colonize. No matter how fast your people grow if there is no space for them on the planet they can't expand the opposite also applies not matter how much space they have if they people can't grow they can't use it anyway.


When designing a race for Thousand Parsec many features about how and where a race lives can be selected. These attributes are used when comparing against a planets attributes to determine the Planet Growth (how fast your people grow on a planet) and Planet Space (how many people you can fit on the planet).


The currently planed attributes that effect Habitability are:


  1. Radiation Band, Optimal Radiation Point and Radiation Band Type

      These values start at 0R (Rads) and go up to 100,000,000R
      This determines how resistant you are to radiation, this includes radiation from the local stars, planetary background radiation and anything else that could produce radiation. Some races might need radiation to survive so there is no bottom.

  2. Gravity Band, Optimal Gravity Point and Gravity Band Type

      These values start at 0G (Gee's) and go up to 100,000G
      This determines how resistant to the forces of gravity. All races start with 0G and go upwards. (Otherwise you can't travel in space can you?)

  3. Temperature Band and Optimal Temperature Point Temperature Band Type

      These values start at 0K (Kelvin) and go up to 10,000K
      This determines how resistant you are to the ravages of temperature.

  4. Population Density

      This value start at 1 centimeter per 1 population unit and go up to 1000 kilometer per population unit.
      This determines the ”density” of your population. High density means aerial bombing does more damage but you can fit more into a smaller space.

  5. Land Type

These value is either Air, Water or Land.
It determines the type of land that your race lives in. Air is the most common and makes Gas Giants naturally habitable, Land is the second most common and Water is fairly rare.

  1. Atmosphere Gases Band

      These are a value for each gas type (Oxygen, Nitrogen, Methane, etc) that are a percentage of the atmosphere
      This determines if your colonists can live in the open or require housing. If they aren't tolerant to the gases in the atmosphere then ”pods” have to be build.

Planet Growth

The planet growth is determined by how well your colonists can survive in the harsh planetary environment. Planet Growth is a ”Percentage ” of your actual Population growth. The actual population growth on a planet is also effected by the percentage space remaining, as there is less space the population will grow slower. More information on this effect can be found in the ”Planet Land” section. Any planet which has a Planet Growth value of less than -100% cannot be landed on. If landing is attempted all colonists will be killed instantly. Planet Growth values between 0% and -100% can be landed on but each year a certain number of colonists will die/be killed/abandon ship each year.


Planets have 3 main attributes, Temperature, Radiation and Gravity. These are the only attributes which determine the Planet Growth value.


Temperature, Radiation and Gravity


A race has a "tolerance" band for each of the main factors. This band give a maximum and minimum values the race can live in. The band is determined by the initial race stats although some technologies can improve the band in certain ways. As well there is an Optimal Point inside each of these bands. This is the value that the race prefers, in Stars! this is the exact middle of the band, TP however lets it be anywhere in the range allowing for races which have high tolerance for hot planets but prefer the cooler ones. This is the value at which the race with grow at 100%. As well unlike Stars! there are also 3 types of bands; linear, exponential positive and exponential negative. Each of these band types are explained below. (All these examples use temperature as the band being looked at but the same applies to both radiation and gravity bands.)


Linear Band

A linear band means that the habitability drops of linearly (I.E a straight line). A very simple way to work out the approximate band factor of say temperature is to do the following.


Take the band, (I.E. From 50K to 500K in this example) and then split it into an above optimal and below optimal. Say the optimal temperature is 150K you would split it into a 50K to 149K band and a 151K to 500K band. Divide this bands by 100, now count outwards toward the planet's actual value, for each block you go past take 1% of the 100% until the value it reached.

Exponential Positive

An exponential positive band means that the band percentage does not drop of linearly, instead as it gets closer to the edges it drops of much faster then if it was near the optimal value. This type of band gives a slow drop off near the center of the band with a fast drop off near the edges. The following method can be used to work out the approximate value of the band percentage.


Take the band (I.E. from 50K to 500K) and then split it into above and below optimal. Say the optimal was 150K, you would split it into a 50K to 149K band and a 151K to 500K band. Then divide the band into two, now take the outermost bit and divide that into two then do the same until you have hundred pieces. Now as you count outwards and take 1% off the 100% until you get to the planets actual temperature.

Planet Space

Planet space determines how many people you have fit on a planet. The biggest factor that determines how many people you can fit on a planet is the races “Land Type”. This value determines weather a race lives in the Air, on the Land or in the Water. Most races that can travel faster then light can easily overcome the problem of not having the correct Land Type using constructions called “Pods”. There are some catches though, pods have to be built so take up resources and minerals, meaning that your population can only expand as fast as you can build pods. You will also have to build pods on planets which don't have an atmosphere that you can tolerate.


Atmosphere

Atmosphere of planets are made up of gases, each race has a tolerance band for each separate gas. There are also two types of bands, the first is “required” this means that your race needs this to breath (an example would be oxygen for humans) the other is “tolerance” this means that your race can breath this without dieing (an example would be nitrogen for humans). If the value is not inside this band the atmosphere is poisonous to your people and you must build pods to house them.


Land Type

There are 3 major land types, Air, Land and Water, most normal planets has a percentage of it's surface covered in Land or Water and 2 radius. The first radius is the Land Radius (for a Gas Giant this would be zero) the second radius is the Air Limit, this is the upper most layer of the atmosphere. For a race to exist on a planet which doesn't have the required land type the race must build pods.


For example, the AquaBabes are Water based creatures, to colonize a Gas Giant they would have to build pods. The GasBags which are Air based creatures could however colonize the planet without the need for pods if they can stand the atmosphere.
